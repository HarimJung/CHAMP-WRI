The Coalition

About CHAMP

A global coalition launched at COP28 to enhance cooperation between national and subnational governments on climate action

The Coalition for High Ambition Multilevel Partnerships (CHAMP) for Climate Action was launched at COP28 in December 2023 by the COP28 Presidency and Bloomberg Philanthropies. It represents a landmark commitment to bridging the gap between national climate pledges and local implementation.

With 78 endorsers — 77 countries plus the European Union — CHAMP covers 36% of the global population, 69% of global GDP, and 40% of global emissions. These endorsers have committed to systematically including cities, states, and regions in their climate policy processes.

The World Resources Institute (WRI) serves as the CHAMP Secretariat, coordinating activities, providing technical support, and facilitating peer learning among endorsing countries.

Commitments

The CHAMP Pledge

1

Integrate subnational perspectives into the development, implementation, and monitoring of Nationally Determined Contributions (NDCs) and National Adaptation Plans (NAPs).

2

Establish or strengthen institutional mechanisms for structured dialogue between national and subnational governments on climate policy.

3

Support subnational governments in accessing climate finance, technical assistance, and capacity-building resources.

4

Improve data collection and reporting systems that incorporate subnational climate action and emissions inventories.

5

Promote peer learning and knowledge sharing between countries on effective multilevel climate governance approaches.

6

Report on progress in enhancing multilevel partnerships for climate action as part of the UNFCCC transparency framework.

Frequently Asked Questions

CHAMP is unique in its focus on multilevel governance — the structured cooperation between national and subnational governments. While other coalitions may focus on emissions targets or specific sectors, CHAMP specifically addresses how different levels of government work together to deliver on climate commitments. This vertical integration is essential because cities and regions are responsible for implementing many national climate policies.

CHAMP endorsement is at the national level, with countries committing to enhance their engagement with subnational governments. However, subnational governments are essential partners and beneficiaries. Organizations representing cities and regions, such as C40, ICLEI, and UCLG, are “Friends of CHAMP” that help facilitate subnational engagement within the coalition framework.

CHAMP endorsers commit to six key areas: integrating subnational perspectives into NDCs, establishing multilevel dialogue mechanisms, supporting subnational access to climate finance, improving data collection, promoting peer learning, and reporting on progress. These commitments are flexible, recognizing that countries have different starting points and governance structures.

CHAMP directly supports the implementation of the Paris Agreement by strengthening how countries develop and deliver on their Nationally Determined Contributions (NDCs). By including subnational governments in NDC processes, countries can set more ambitious and achievable targets. CHAMP endorsers report on their multilevel partnership progress as part of the UNFCCC transparency framework.
